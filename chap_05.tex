\chapter{Release 3: Payments, Teacher Tools and Dashboard}

\section*{Introduction}

Release 3 implemented the commercial and operational aspects of the LMS: cart, orders, payment integration, teacher content management tools and the administrative dashboard for platform operators.

\section{Sprint 5: Payments, orders and vendor management}

\subsection{Sprint objectives}
Enable students to purchase courses using a secure checkout flow, support coupon discounts and produce order records that can be inspected from the admin dashboard.

\subsection{Backlog (examples)}

\hline
1 & Shopping cart model and APIs & 1 & 3 \\
\hline
2 & Order creation, coupon application and invoice generation & 1 & 3 \\
\hline
3 & Stripe checkout integration and payment success handling & 1 & 4 \\
\hline
\end{longtable}

\section{Sprint 6: Teacher features and administrative dashboard}

\subsection{Sprint objectives}
Provide teachers with a simple authoring interface to create and manage courses, coupons and respond to student questions. Provide admins with aggregated statistics and operational controls via the dashboard.

\subsection{Backlog (examples)}
\begin{longtable}{|m{1cm}|m{8cm}|m{2cm}|m{2cm}|}
\hline
1 & Teacher course create/update endpoints and frontend UI & 1 & 4 \\
\hline
2 & Dashboard statistics (enrollments, revenue, pending approvals) & 1 & 3 \\
\hline
3 & Notification and coupon management for teachers & 2 & 2 \\
\hline
\end{longtable}

\section*{Conclusion}

With Release 3 the platform obtains full end-to-end commerce flows and teacher/admin tooling. The system is ready for further improvements such as production-grade media storage, improved reporting and additional learning features (quizzes, grading, certificates).
